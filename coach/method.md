# NORWEGIAN SINGLES APPROACH (NSA)
*Sustainable Sub-Threshold Training for Hobby Joggers*

---

## METHODOLOGY OVERVIEW

The Norwegian Singles Approach (NSA) is a time-efficient application of Norwegian training principles designed for runners with 6–8 hours of weekly training time. Unlike elite "double threshold" methods, this approach focuses on **one quality session per day** ("singles") to maximize aerobic development without the injury risk or burnout associated with high-intensity anaerobic work.

### Core Framework

1.  **High Frequency Quality**: 3 Sub-Threshold sessions + 4 Easy runs weekly.
2.  **Controlled Intensity**: Quality work is strictly **Sub-Threshold** (2.5–3.5 mmol/L lactate), not "hard" threshold.
3.  **Flat Loading**: Replaces "boom-bust" periodization (build/peak/taper) with a consistent, repeatable weekly load.
4.  **Strict Discipline**: Easy runs must be truly easy (<70% Max HR) to allow for 3 quality days.


---

## PHYSIOLOGICAL TARGETS

The goal is to accumulate time in the "sweet spot" to push the aerobic threshold up, rather than stressing the anaerobic system.

| Zone | Metric | Feeling | Goal |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Easy** | **<70% Max HR** | Conversational, nasal breathing | Recovery, volume accumulation |
| **Sub-Threshold** | **2.5 – 3.5 $mmol/L$** | Comfortably hard, controlled | Maximize aerobic load without accumulation of fatigue |
| **Anaerobic** | **>4.0 $mmol/L$** | Burning legs, gasping | **AVOID** (Eliminated from this method) |

**Key Differentiator**: This method deliberately eliminates VO2max intervals, neuromuscular speed work, and hill repeats to ensure the aerobic system is the primary limiter.

## WEEKLY STRUCTURE

The NSA uses a repetitive "flat week" structure. Every week looks identical to build a Chronic Training Load (CTL) efficiently.

| Day | Session Type | Duration | Protocol |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mon** | Easy Run | 45-60 min | <70% Max HR |
| **Tue** | **Sub-Threshold** | 50-60 min | **Short Intervals** (e.g., 10-12 x 1000m) |
| **Wed** | Easy Run | 45-60 min | <70% Max HR |
| **Thu** | **Sub-Threshold** | 50-60 min | **Medium Intervals** (e.g., 4-6 x 2000m) |
| **Fri** | Easy Run | 45-60 min | <70% Max HR |
| **Sat** | **Sub-Threshold** | 50-60 min | **Long Intervals** (e.g., 3-4 x 3000m) |
| **Sun** | **Long Run** | 75-90 min | Easy pace (can drift slightly >70% HR at end) |

## SESSION PROTOCOLS

### 1. Sub-Threshold Intervals
*Target: 20–25% of weekly volume.*

**Primary target: HR Zone 4 (160-168).** All interval sessions are governed by heart rate, not pace. Pace is recorded for post-session analysis and fitness tracking, but never used as the in-run target. GPS pace is unreliable (especially with consumer watches); HR reflects actual physiological effort. Sub-threshold is a metabolic state — run by HR and feel, analyze pace after.

* **Short Intervals (1K):**
    * **Format:** 10–12 x 1000m
    * **Target:** Z4 HR (160-168)
    * **Rest:** 60 seconds standing/walking
    * **Feel:** Comfortably hard. Should feel like you could do 2-3 more reps at the end.

* **Medium Intervals (2K):**
    * **Format:** 4–6 x 2000m
    * **Target:** Z4 HR (160-168)
    * **Rest:** 60–90 seconds
    * **Feel:** Controlled effort. Same intensity across all reps.

* **Long Intervals (3K):**
    * **Format:** 3–4 x 3000m
    * **Target:** Z4 HR (160-168)
    * **Rest:** 90 seconds
    * **Feel:** Sustained tempo.

### 2. Easy Runs
*Target: 75–80% of weekly volume.*

* **Intensity:** Strictly under 70% of Maximum Heart Rate (Z1: < 144).
* **Pace:** Whatever keeps HR in Z1. Pace is irrelevant — if HR is < 144, the run is easy regardless of speed.
* **Purpose:** To allow recovery for the next day's sub-threshold work. If you run these too fast, the method fails.

## EXECUTION & PROGRESSION

### The "Flat Load" Philosophy
Instead of building for 3 weeks and recovering for 1, find a load you can repeat indefinitely.
* **Week 1-2:** Focus on completion. Run slower than you think you need to.
* **Week 3-8 (The Doubt Phase):** You may feel stagnant. **Do not increase intensity.** Adaptations take 6–8 weeks to manifest.
* **Week 9+:** You will likely experience a "breakthrough" where the same heart rate yields a significantly faster pace.

### Marathon Adjustments (12 Weeks Out)
The standard NSA structure scales to the marathon with minimal changes:
1.  **Long Run:** Gradually extend to ~2.5 hours (match race duration).
2.  **Specific Intensity:** Convert one Sub-Threshold session (usually Saturday) to Marathon Pace work (e.g., 5 x 5000m @ Goal MP).
3.  **Volume:** Total hours may increase slightly (e.g., 7 $\rightarrow$ 7.5 hours), but the structure remains flat.

## COMMON ERRORS TO AVOID

1.  **Turning it into 80/20:** Do not introduce VO2max or "hard" speed work. The 25% quality must be strictly sub-threshold.
2.  **Running Easy Runs too Fast:** If your easy runs are at 75% HR, you will carry fatigue into the sub-threshold sessions and fail to absorb the training.
3.  **Chasing Pace vs. Physiology:** Sub-threshold is a metabolic state (lactate), not a fixed speed. Slow down in heat, humidity, or fatigue to maintain the correct effort level.
4.  **Judging by "Suffering":** Workouts should not leave you destroyed. Success is measured by the ability to repeat the session 48 hours later, not by exhaustion.

> **Mantra:** *"Find the highest training load you can handle indefinitely, then back off 10% so you're not constantly on the edge."*
